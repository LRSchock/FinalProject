Those who live in apartments are typically leasing from a landlord. Under these circumstances, the landlord may be held accountable for injuries sustained in common areas or injuries that resulted from inadequate maintenance of the individual’s apartment, as well as common areas.

Typical actions arising out of premises liability include injuries that result from fires or explosions, hot water heater injuries, inadequate security, slips and falls, roof top injuries, lead paint exposure, carbon monoxide poisoning, among others.

Fire & Explosion Injuries

While these types of accidents can happen in any type of dwelling, if you were invited onto someone else’s property, you may be able to make a claim for damages against the property owner.

Hot Water Heater Injuries

Hot water heater accidents are common in apartments. While the proper operation of fixtures will likely fall under the person who is leasing the apartment, the hot water heater is managed by the apartment complex.

The New York State Department of Health requires that water heaters be set at 120 degree Fahrenheit. Anything above this temperature is considered to be dangerous. Exposure to water temperatures of 140 degrees can cause severe burns. The most common causes of hot water accidents are faulty regulators. However, other causes include improper inspections, insufficient water pressure, and electrical malfunctions.

Report faulty heaters to the landlord. Those who are injured as the result of scalding, may be able to file a claim against their landlord. A landlord can be held responsible if they fail to set the water heater at the proper temperature. In the case of a defective water heater, depending on where the fault lies, you may be able to make a claim against the landlord, maintenance company, or even the manufacturer, or a combination of these.

Legionnaire’s Disease

Similar to hot water claims, the landlord is responsible for maintaining a safe water supply in the building. Recently, there have been several incidents of legionnaire’s disease in New York City that were transmitted through an apartment or public building’s water supply. If you or a family member contracted legionnaire’s disease, contact an attorney immediately.

Rooftop Accidents and Injuries

In New York City, a rooftop exit is a necessary fire escape option for tall buildings. In addition, there has been an increase in the number of rooftop pools, nightclubs and bars in recent years. Unfortunately, there have also been a number of deaths and injuries that can be attributed to improper safety and security precautions taken with respect to rooftops.

Some examples of claims that have arisen due to rooftop negligence include:

  • wrongful death resulting from being thrown or falling off a rooftop with inadequate railings.
  • Batteries, sex assaults and rapes resulting from a lack of rooftop security.
