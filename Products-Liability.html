Product Liability

New York Product Liability Lawyers



As consumers, we expect that the products we purchase will be safe to use. However, when a dangerous or defective product enters the stream of commerce, the results can be catastrophic for consumers and bystanders, resulting in thousands of injuries and deaths each year.

Product Liability is the specialized area of law that deals with the liability of the manufacturer, wholesaler, or retailer of a product for injuries resulting from that product. Defective product cases can be quite complicated with several people and parties involved in the production, distribution and sale of the defective product.

Some common defective or harmful product cases include children’s toys, household and landscaping tools, automobiles, medical devices, pharmaceuticals, industrial machines, as well as food products. Manufacturers, wholesalers and retailers may all be responsible for injuries that are caused as a result of a defective product. If products contain a manufacturing flaw, a design defect, or an inadequate warning they are considered to be defective. 

In defective product cases it is absolutely essential that measures be taken immediately to preserve evidence so that qualified engineers and other experts can determine the nature and cause of the defect.  Thus it is imperative that the purchaser of the product take the following measures immediately:

  • Comprehensive Estate Plans – includes the creation of a will, trusts, power of attorney, health care proxy, and beneficiary designation.
  • Living Trust – provides lifetime and after-death asset management to avoid probate.
  • Supplemental Needs Trust – designed to benefit a person with a mental or physical disability.
  • Irrevocable Life Insurance Trust (ILIT) – takes ownership of a life insurance policy to reduce estate tax liability.
  • Strategies to Reduce or Eliminate Estate Tax – crucial for individuals whose estate will be taxable at the state and/or federal level.
  • Review and Revision of Wills and Estate Planning Documents – ensure documents are up-to-date with recent changes in the law and tax code.

In addition to estate planning, we also provide expertise in the following related areas of practice:

  • Seek medical attention
  • Save the receipts and instruction manuals and the product parts (if possible)
  • Take photos of the defective product and the injuries suffered
  • Contact an attorney

Some injuries caused by defective products are injuries to the eyes and hands, amputations, cuts, scars, burns, poison by ingestion or inhalation, fractures, sprains, broken bones, nerve damage and other injuries. At the Law Offices Ross, Legan, Rosenberg, Zelen & Flaks, LLP, our product liability attorneys maintain a network with medical consultants and engineers who will assist in assessing the nature and extent of your injuries and assessing the defect in the product that caused your injury.

When you or someone you know is injured by a defective product, it is crucial that you contact a product liability attorney immediately.
