If you or a loved one is hurt in a premises accident you should try to take photographs of the dangerous condition as soon as possible and make sure that the accident is reported to someone on the premises and to the Police. 

In order to be successful in a personal injury claim based on premises liability, the individual or institution that you are filing a claim against must have actually owned the premises at the time the accident occurred, unless the premises contained an “attractive nuisance” to a child, such as a swimming pool. If so, then you must also consider whether or not the person making the claim was permitted to be at the premises at the time the accident. Additionally, the property owner must have been negligent. If each of these elements are met, you may be able make a clam for premises liability. However, you should contact an attorney quickly since there may be strict time limits for which to make a claim or start a lawsuit.
